Way of the Friar

"One with staff and shield
Paragon of the people
Guardian of light"

Lore 3rd Level - Subclass Selection 6th Level 9th Level 11th Level 17th Level

Lore

"Where many monastic paths bend inward, the Friars I encountered in this dwarven village are drawn to lead. I have watched them at first light doing what they can; mending, teaching and guiding long before a blade is ever drawn. Perhaps most impressive is that they bear Lathander's radiance as a lantern, not a lance. When violence comes, they step between, staves poised and shields raised. They are not pacifists; while they choose to protect, they are formidable in their own right. I set this down not as flattery but as a record: courage can be quiet and humility can be bold."

The Psyker

"Patron of the warp
Promises of such power
Rewards of chaos

Lore 1st Level - Subclass Selection 6th Level 10th Level 14th Level

Lore

"Not long after the battle had ended, I was summoned to observe the questioning of a warlock we had taken alive. He was one of those wielding a power unknown to us, a force that bent past our strongest wards as if they were cobwebs. Our clerics could not pierce his thoughts, nor could the magi hold his gaze for long—madness curled into their minds like smoke, forcing them to look away.

I chose another path. I spoke with him not as a captor, but as a scholar. At first his words were halting, as if weighed down by exhaustion, but before long he poured out his tale. He spoke of walking the edge of oblivion, of the great lighthouse in the dark he only knew by its luster. Each time he called upon that beacon, it demanded more from him—mind, body, spirit alike—until his voice shook with equal parts reverence and torment.

As he raved, his eyes shone with that same lightless malevolence I had glimpsed on the field. His words quickened, frayed at the edges, and then—without warning—his form erupted in a pillar of unholy flame. We staggered back, blinded and horrified, as he was consumed before us. I was left aghast, my heart thundering with one question only: what power had we truly come against?"

Circle of Wrath

"A calamity:
Nature forced to reply with
Overwhelming wrath"

Lore 2nd Level - Subclass Selection 6th Level 10th Level 14th Level

Lore

"We did not know who would hear the heavenly call to battle and certainly did not expect the druids to leave their wards in search of valor. The land we came upon, however, had been ravaged by the demons pouring out from the wound and with such vile delight. After clearing out a village of demonspawn, we encountered a circle of druids offering their might as agents of nature's wrath. Any committed to the cause were welcome but no one had ever seen or heard of a druid circle with the spirit of destruction behind them and so we whispered and wondered.

Perhaps perceiving our curiosity, their leader told tales of the aspects of wrath and the similarities to our own were staggering! Whatever names they had for these forces held no weight against the truest of their description: Kossuth, the blessed flame. Auril, the frozen death. Talos, the raging storm. Grumbar the might of force. I asked for a demonstration as I tend to do but she warned me against such a display; to summon the winds of destruction is to invite it within, to be consumed by the rage of the wild, only a last resort…

They had come to the right place."

3rd Level – Subclass Selection

Conduit of the Weave

Passive
When a levelled spell is cast within 18 m, you can use your reaction to siphon some of its energy, restoring a Sorcery Point. You can hold up to 1 + your Intelligence modifier Sorcery Points that replenish on a Long Rest. Both this reaction and any other casting features require a free offhand and at most Light Armor.
